## 1. Function of a Complex Variable – Definition

A **function of a complex variable** is a rule that assigns **one complex number** to **each complex number** in its domain.

\[
w = f(z)
\]
where  
\[
z = x + iy,\quad w = u + iv
\]

Here,  
\[
u = u(x,y),\quad v = v(x,y)
\]

---

## 2. Tamil Meaning (தமிழ் விளக்கம்)

ஒரு **சிக்கலெண் செயல்பாடு (Function of Complex Variable)** என்பது,  
ஒவ்வொரு சிக்கலெண்ணிற்கும் ஒரு சிக்கலெண்ணை இணைக்கும் விதியாகும்.

\[
z = x + iy \Rightarrow f(z) = u(x,y) + iv(x,y)
\]

---

## 3. Real and Imaginary Parts

For  
\[
f(z) = u(x,y) + iv(x,y)
\]

- **Real part:** \( u(x,y) \)
- **Imaginary part:** \( v(x,y) \)

---

## 4. Examples of Functions of Complex Variable

1. \( f(z) = z^2 \)

\[
= (x+iy)^2 = (x^2 - y^2) + i(2xy)
\]

2. \( f(z) = \bar{z} = x - iy \)

3. \( f(z) = |z|^2 = x^2 + y^2 \)

---

## 5. Constant and Identity Functions

- **Constant function:**  
\[
f(z) = c
\]

- **Identity function:**  
\[
f(z) = z
\]

---

## 6. Mapping (Transformation)

A **mapping** is a function that **transforms points in the z-plane** into **points in the w-plane**.

\[
w = f(z)
\]

---

## 7. Tamil Meaning (Mapping)

**மாப்பிங் (Mapping)** என்பது  
\( z \)-தளத்தில் உள்ள புள்ளிகளை  
\( w \)-தளத்தில் உள்ள புள்ளிகளாக மாற்றும் செயல்.

---

## 8. Types of Elementary Mappings

---

### (a) Translation
\[
w = z + c
\]

**Effect:** Shifts the figure without changing shape.

---

### (b) Rotation
\[
w = e^{i\theta} z
\]

**Effect:** Rotates figure through angle \( \theta \).

---

### (c) Magnification
\[
w = kz,\quad k>1
\]

**Effect:** Enlarges the figure.

---

### (d) Reflection
\[
w = \bar{z}
\]

**Effect:** Reflection about x-axis.

---

## 9. Mapping of Straight Lines and Circles

General form:
\[
|z - a| = r
\]

Under mapping:
\[
w = f(z)
\]

- Straight lines may map to straight lines or circles
- Circles may map to circles or straight lines

---

## 10. Important Theorem

### Theorem:
**Every linear transformation maps circles and lines into circles or lines.**

---

## 11. Proof (Outline)

Let
\[
w = az + b,\quad a \neq 0
\]

General equation of circle/line:
\[
\alpha z\bar{z} + \beta z + \bar{\beta}\bar{z} + \gamma = 0
\]

Substituting \( z = \frac{w-b}{a} \),  
we again obtain an equation of a circle or line in \( w \)-plane.

Hence proved.

---

## 12. Examples on Mapping

### Example 1:
Find the image of the line \( x = 2 \) under  
\[
w = z + 1
\]

**Solution:**  
Line shifts to \( x = 3 \)

---

### Example 2:
Image of circle \( |z| = 1 \) under  
\[
w = 2z
\]

**Answer:**  
\[
|w| = 2
\]

---

## 13. Important Results

1. Mapping preserves angle only in conformal mappings.
2. Linear transformations preserve shape.
3. Magnitude and rotation can be combined in \( az \).

---

## 14. Formula Summary

\[
w = z + c \Rightarrow \text{Translation}
\]

\[
w = az \Rightarrow \text{Rotation + Scaling}
\]

\[
w = \bar{z} \Rightarrow \text{Reflection}
\]
